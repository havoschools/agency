    Need an eCommerce website?

    Sell stuff with an eCom website your customers love.

    Get ready to hear that "cha-ching". 

    Take your online store from drab to fab with an eCommerce website where the cart practically fills itself...

    Give your customers and digital window shoppers an incredible online experience — one that keeps them coming back for more.


    Whether you've got a few niche products in your spare room or a whole warehouse of goods, we'll set you up for commercial success with an easy-to-use eCommerce site.


    ﻿Depending on your business needs, preferences, and store complexity, your new eCom site can be built in either WordPress or Duda.

    • Who will build my new website?

      You'll have a dedicated team working for you on the day. This includes a professional Designer, Copywriter, and Account Manager — the dream team. Sometimes we throw a Developer in there too, if your website build is a little more complex. 


      The Account Coordinater will be your primary point of in the lead up to the day and on the day. They manage the rest of the team, talk broader marketing strategies with you (if you like), answer any questions you have, and generally support you throughout the day wherever you need.


      The Copywriter creates all the written content for your site. Sometimes, business owners provide a rough draft for us to work with, other times, our Copywriter creates it all from scratch. Either way, they'll ensure your content is catchy, engaging, and enjoyable to read — and encourages people to get in touch with you.


      The Designer makes your website look fresh and engaging, and ensures it reflect your brand. It'll be simple to use and edit, and website visitors will easily be able to navigate the site to find what they need. They'll make your website pop with cool animations, eye-catching fonts, crisp imagery, and slick colour combos — always utilising modern design trends and best practices. 


      The Developer helps with any technical stuff that falls outside the expertise of our other three pros. Often, we don't actually call on our Developer, but we'd like to let you know that we can if we need to.


      To see who exactly could be working on your website project, check out our People Page.

    • What happens in the lead up to the day?

      Get started by signing up online. You'll be asked to pay a 50% deposit, which secures your booking. After that, you'll be linked to a simple online briefing form where you can tell us all about your business.


      When your briefing form comes thorugh, we'll match you with one of our Account Managers who will personally oversee and coordinate your website project. The match is based on the personal interests and past experience of the Account Manager, as well as the industry of your business. Pretty cool! They'll reach out to you to introduce themselves, confirm the booking, and ask any additional questions about your business. You can ask them questions, too.

    • What happens on the day?

      We'll start with a 9:00am briefing meeting. Usually, this happens in our board room, but if you're doing your One Day Website remote, it'll be a video call. This meeting is a chance for us to get to know one another. We'll go over that initial briefing form you sent through in more detail and ask any additional questions. You can share your hopes for the website, broading business goals, and ask us anything, too.


      Then, your Copywriter and Designer will get started on an awesome Home Page concept for you. We'll check in, get feedback, and make adjustment as needed until you're obsessed with it. Once the Home Page is approved, this really sets the tone for the rest of the site, and it all comes together pretty quickly. Throughout the day, we'll continue checking in for feedback — you can make as many changes as you like on the day as guide the design and written content as you see fit.


      Once all five pages are complete, our Designer sits down with you for a one-on-one training session where they'll show you how to edit and update your website. Don't worry — it's super easy!


      That's basically it. When you're ready, we'll make the website live and you're good to go. You can make your website live on the same day, or give us the nod when you're ready down the track if you have a few other business bits and pieces to sort out before the launch.

    • What if it doesn’t get finished on time?

      It's very unlikely that your website won't be finished by 5:00pm. In fact, most of our websits wrap up even earlier than that! 


      However, it does happen (very rarely). This comes down to the level of complexity of the site, and if there were many unexpected, significant changes throughout the day. We're talking complete redesigns, massive writing edits, or other big changes late in the day. 


      The good news is, if your build flows onto the next day, more often than not, there is no additional charge (we'll let you know if this isn't the case). As we said at the start, this is extremely uncommon. Out of our hundreds of One Day Website builds, this had only happened a handful of times. And when it did happen, it was usually completed by midday the next day. 

    • Do I have to build the website in just one day?

      No. If you'd like to purchase the One Day Website package and roll it out over a long period, that's absolutely fine. Note that this is not the same as your One Day Website not being finished in a single day (as covered in the previous point).


      What we mean is, you're welcome to purchase a One Day Website package as request it actually be completed over a week or a month (but no more than three months). The process remains exactly the same, just spread out over a longer period. We just need a concrete deadline from you so we know what end date to work towards.

    Website terminology.

    • One page

      When talking about the size of a website, we refer to it by the number of "pages" it contains. If your eCom site had 8 pages, these might be:

      1. Home
      2. About
      3. Giving Back
      4. Shop
      5. Blog
      6. Terms and Conditions
      7. Privacy Policy
      8. Contact

      The exact pages you want are up to you. If you'd like some help, we can advise you on the pages we think would be best.

    • Sitemap

      Following on from the last point, a collection of pages is called a "sitemap". Using the same example, your complete sitemap for your website might be:

      1. Home
      2. About
      3. Giving Back
      4. Shop
      5. Blog
      6. Terms and Conditions
      7. Privacy Policy
      8. Contact

      It goes without saying that your sitemap is totally up to you. Structuring your website and sitemap actually falls into the expertise of our Copywriter, so we can definitely assist here if needed. 

    • Content Management System — Duda & WordPress

      A "Content Management System" — also known as CMS — its the software used to build and edit the website. For example, an eCommerce website can be built in Duda or WordPress. Both are built without code, which means it's easy for you to use and edit after the build is complete.

    • SEO

      SEO stands for Search Engine Optimisation. It's the process of getting your website picked up by search engines, like Google, with the goal of appearing on the first page of search engine results. SEO is an extremely time intensive (but worth it) marketing strategy that takes months of work before you start seeing results. 


      However, we implement basic SEO during your eCom build to help you get a head start. We can also provide ongoing SEO to help your shop stand out on Google.
