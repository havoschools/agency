    What is PPC?

    PPC stands for "Pay Per Click". It describes digital advertising on a variety of platforms, such as Google Ads (same as Google Adwords), Facebook Ads, Instagram Ads, LinkedIn Ads, and Microsoft Ads.


    PPC trades uses auctioning system where you bid on certain placements, such as at the top of Google or in the sidebar of Facebook. You secure this placement by outbidding your competitors (rather, the platform you choose automatically bids for you within the parameters you set). You're only charged when someone actually clicks on your ad (not just when they see it), which is why this form of digital advertising is called PPC.



    One of the reasons businesses love PPC is because it's a fast way to get in front of potential customers. Unlike         SEO (which is very effective but takes a long time), PPC advertising provides fast results — as soon as the same day they're launched.

    • Landing Pages

      Be honest, were you planning to use Google Ads to send people to your home page? Bad idea.


      When someone sees your Google Ad or Facebook Ad (or whatever ad) it's because they've searched for something specific, and it’s not to generally learn more about your business. For example, say you search for PPC Management Perth and click on an amazing Google Ad for exactly that (perhaps you actually did?). Would you prefer to land on this page that talks exclusively about PPC management for Perth businesses, or would you prefer to land on our home page where we lay out everything we do? The latter would be pretty annoying, so we always do the former. 


      Give people the content they are looking for by directing them to a purpose-built landing page. Our strategically created landing pages serve the content your potential customers are craving on a silver platter. 


      Technical notes for the discerning: dedicated landing pages increase landing page experience, click through rate, and ad relevancy. Optimising these metrics increases conversion rates and lowers cost per conversion. It may also help with your SEO.

    • Google Ads: Search Campaigns

      You know those little boxes that appear at the top of Google when you search for a service or product? And they somehow know exactly what you are wanting, so you click on them and spend some money? We facilitate that process. 


      We work hard to can get your businesses ad at the top of a relevant Google search. Then, we keep showing your ad to the right people until they take action.


      We do Microsoft Ads too… Assuming there is enough of your potential customers using that search engine.

    • Google Display Ads

      Have you ever noticed that when you start talking about a particular product or service, you start seeing ads for it? That’s our fault, sorry (but also, not sorry). 


      These are called "Display Ads" — they're eye-catching ads about your service. Once designed, the greater powers that be (Google and Facebook) decide who needs to see that ad, based on a user’s behavior and interests. Creepy? Absoultey. But it's a totally legit tool that you can use to get in front of potential customers.


      And yeah, we have in-house graphic designers who design these for no extra cost.

    • Google Ads Remarketing

      Ever felt like a business is everywhere, following you online wherever you go? No, they aren’t omnipresent, they're simply appearing for you thanks to our friend, the pixel.


      A pixel is a tiny, invisible piece of code that sits on your website and latches to prospective customer the moment they go to your website. It will then follow them around the web and show them ads for your business until they eventually spend with you. Pretty cool, hey?

    • Facebook & Instagram Ads

      Social media ads can be a very powerful driver to generate interest and sales for your business. In particular, we are talking about Facebook Ads and Instagram Ads (which both run out of Facebook Ads, since FB owns Insta).


      These platforms offer precise, relevant targeting tools so we can place your ads in front of your target market. We can define your ideal audience in terms of:

      • Demographics (age, income, gender, marital status, etc).
      • Psychographics (beliefs, values, interests, etc).
      • Behaviour (this person liked this page, or went to this type of place, etc).

      We often recommend Facebook Ads when our clients have a service their customers don’t know they need (and are therefore not searching for it in Google). Here's an extreme example, based on a real client. They wanted to sell a kid's hair brush with unicorn handle, but of course, no one was searching "unicorn handle hair brush" in Google. However, this client had clear demographic (parents of kids in a certain age bracket), so we created Facebook Ads showing how cool this hairbrush was. Boom — once parent's saw it, they thought "my kid would love that!", and sales started pouring in.


      Social media as help create a demand for your product by putting it in front of the people you know will want it, even if they don't know they want it (yet). We've discovered that this platform works really well for certain industries, such as fitness, photography, hospitality, lifestyle, and driving schools, to name a few.

And out of this already small selection of people (somtimes down to the hundreds), how many of them are actively using the platform enough to see your ad? And out of those people, how many are in a position to engage your services. Probably zero.
