    Why Google Ads? 

    Google Ads (previously Google AdWords) is a digital marketing platform that lets you show advertisements on Google search results pages. When someone searches a phrase related to your advertised services, they'll be shown your ad.

    Firstly, you might be wondering what the difference between Google Ads and Google AdWords is. Google AdWords first launched as a marketing platform in 2000. In 2018, this platform underwent a rebranding in which the name was changed to Google Ads - but rest assured, the marketing potential remains!


    Google Ads is a form of Pay Per Click (PPC) marketing, in which you pay only when a potential customer clicks the link to your ad. PPC and digital marketing have levelled the playing field for small businesses - you no longer need to invest hundreds of thousands of dollars into a marketing campaign for it to be successful. Instead, Google Ads presents a cost-effective way to generate leads and start seeing results fast.


    One of the major benefits to Google Ads is that your ads only show up to people searching for related phrases - which means your audience is almost guaranteed to be interested in your services, and motivated to buy. You can even split your ads up based on different services, so each individual advert will target a specific Google search. This allows you to target a wide range of potential customers who might be looking for slightly different services.

    • Google Search Ads

      Google Search Ads are the bread and butter of Google Ads – they're efficient, they get the job done, and they’re used almost universally. When you search for a service or product in Google, these are the ads that show at the top of the results page.  


      These ads are a great way of generating traffic to your website, and getting motivated customers looking at your services. Since they’ve already searched a phrase related to your business, these customers are actively looking for your services and are likely going to be ready to buy. 


      While the most common use of Google Search Ads is to advertise products or services, they’re also great for brand awareness. Think of it this way – if someone is searching for your brand name, don’t you want to make sure you’re the top result they find? With Google Search Ads, we can direct people who are searching for your brand name to your website, and make sure they don’t find a competitor’s website instead. 


      Google Search Ads are usually the first type of ad we’ll set up for you, as they tend to generate fast results and high-quality leads.  

    • Google Display Ads

      These are the ads that pop up on the side of websites, or in blog articles. They’re designed to be eye-catching, and usually feature an image, gif, or video about your business. These ads are placed by Google into different web pages, and can reach a broad audience at a fairly low price point. 


      Our in-house design team have years of experience creating striking graphics for these ads, at no extra cost to you. 

    • Google Ads Remarketing

      You know those ads that seem to follow you around across the web? Those are remarketing ads, and they’re perfect for getting sales from customers who have already visited your website. 


      Maybe a customer clicked on one of your ads, but they weren’t ready to buy anything at that point. Rather than let them forget about you, we can set up remarketing Google Ads to remind them of how great your services are and how much they need them!  


      Google Ads uses code to determine which users have visited your website, and then tracks them as they move around the web. This gives you a whole audience of potential customers who are primed to spend money with your business – you just have to tap into it. 


      Google Display Ads are usually a good option for Google Remarketing campaigns, since they give a quick visual reminder of your services. 

    • Landing Pages

      Landing page optimisation is vital to the success of your Google Ads. At no extra cost, we’ll create new landing pages which are optimised to encourage customers to buy. Our in-house graphic designers will create a brand new page specifically for your ads, and our copywriter will write a compelling description of your services designed to encourage customers to buy. 


      It’s long been proven that when a landing page is closely aligned with an ad, customers are more likely to engage with it. It makes sense – if you click on an advert for a service, you don’t want to be taken to an unrelated page. When we design landing pages, they give your potential customers the information that they’re actually looking for, and an easy way for them to pay for your services. 

    • Conversion Tracking

      To monitor the success of a Google Ads campaign, it’s important to be able to determine when your customers are converting. In Google Ads talk, “conversions” are any action that you want someone to take after they click on an ad – whether it be filling in a contact form, booking an appointment, or purchasing a product.  


      At Helium, we believe in data-driven results, and we set up and manage conversion tracking for your Google Ads. This way, we’re able to tell what’s working and what’s not.

    • Do I really need a new landing page?

      We offer landing page creation as a service because we find that specially-designed landing pages make customers more likely to convert. However, depending on your website, you might not actually need one.  


      There are several factors necessary to make your existing website page an effective landing page. These factors can be more difficult to control in a website than in an individual, custom-built landing page. Your website will need to be well designed and trusted by Google. Further, it will need to have a fast loading time – customers are impatient, and if they have to wait around for your webpage to load, they’re more likely to just leave the page. It will also need to have a page that is well suited to your specific advert. If you have all of these factors – great! You probably don’t need a custom landing page. 


      If you’re reading this and realising that your website doesn’t have an ideal landing page, that’s where we come in. It can be time consuming and difficult to address all of these factors when editing an entire website. We’ve found that it’s usually more efficient to create an entirely new page, which we can ensure will load quickly and provide all the information that customers need to convert. 

    • Should I use Facebook Ads instead of Google Ads?

      Google Ads are ideal way of getting an audience that is actively seeking out your services, and is ready to convert. We’ve found greater success using Google Ads than Facebook Ads across almost all of our clients, which is why we recommend it as the first PPC marketing option to look into. 


      However, there are some clients who do see results from Facebook Ads. This difference usually has to do with the specific services being offered, and what audiences you’re trying to target. 


      We can advise you on whether we think your business will benefit from Facebook Ads once we know more about your services. If you’re interested in Facebook Ads, we usually recommend using them together with Google Ads, rather than setting up Facebook Ads alone. 

    • How long does it take to start seeing results?

      One of the benefits of Google Ads is that they tend to start generating results quickly. Compared to Search Engine Optimisation, Google Ads are an effective way to quickly find new, interested customers. 


      After they are first set up, Google Ads must pass an approval process. This review doesn’t usually take more than one day. After this, they’ll be live – and people will start seeing them!  


      For around a week after the ads are live, they’ll be in a learning period. This is when Google collects data from your ads to figure out which ads to show to potential customers, and which specific combination of headlines and descriptions generates the best results. During this time, you should slowly see an increase in conversions. After this learning period, your ads will be optimised by Google and you'll start seeing the bulk of inquiries.
