    • What happens in the lead up to the build?

      Get started by signing up online. You'll be asked to pay a 50% deposit, which secures your booking. After that, you'll be linked to a simple online briefing form where you can tell us all about your business.


      When your briefing form comes thorugh, we'll match you with one of our Account Managers who will personally oversee and coordinate your website project.     • If I choose a One Day Website, what happens on the day?

      We'll start with a 9:00am briefing meeting. Usually, this happens in our boardroom, but if you're doing your One Day Website remote, it'll be a video call. This meeting is a chance for us to get to know one another. We'll go over that initial briefing form you sent through in more detail and ask any additional questions. You can share your hopes for the website, broading business goals, and ask us anything, too.


      Then, your Copywriter and Designer will get started on an awesome Home Page concept for you. We'll check in, get feedback, and make adjustment as needed until you're obsessed with it. Once the Home Page is approved, this really sets the tone for the rest of the site, and it all comes together pretty quickly. Throughout the day, we'll continue checking in for feedback — you can make as many changes as you like on the day as guide the design and written content as you see fit.


      Once all five pages are complete, our Designer sits down with you for a one-on-one training session where they'll show you how to edit and update your website. Don't worry — it's super easy!


      That's basically it. When you're ready, we'll make the website live and you're good to go. You can make your website live on the same day, or give us the nod when you're ready down the track if you have a few other business bits and pieces to sort out before the launch.

    • What if my One Day Website doesn’t get finished on time?

      It's very unlikely that your website won't be finished by 5:00pm. In fact, most of our websites wrap up even earlier than that! 


      However, it does happen (very rarely). This comes down to the level of complexity of the site, and if there were many unexpected, significant changes throughout the day. We're talking complete redesigns, massive writing edits, or other big changes late in the day. Delays also happen if we're missing key information from you (e.g. staff bios or crucial details about your services). 


      The good news is, if your build flows onto the next day, more often than not, there is no additional charge (we'll let you know if this isn't the case). As we said at the start, this is extremely uncommon. Out of our hundreds of One Day Website builds, this had only happened a handful of times. And when it did happen, it was usually completed by midday the next day.

    Website terminology.

    • One page

      When talking about the size of a website, we refer to it by the number of "pages" it contains. Your One Day Website includes five pages. As an example, these might be:

      1. Home
      2. About
      3. Services
      4. Blog
      5. Contact

      The exact pages you want are up to you. If you'd like some help, we can advise you on the pages we think would be best.

    • Sitemap

      Following on from the last point, a collection of pages is called a "sitemap". Using the same example, your complete sitemap for your website might be:

      1. Home
      2. About
      3. Services
      4. Blog
      5. Contact

      It goes without saying that your sitemap is totally up to you. Structuring your website and sitemap actually falls into the expertise of our Copywriter, so we can definitely assist here if needed. 

    • Content Management System

      A "Content Management System" — also known as CMS — its the software used to build and edit the website. For example, all our One Day Websites are built in Duda. However, larger website can also be built by us in WordPress. Both are built without code, which means it's easy for you to use and edit after the build is complete.

    • Duda

      Duda is the Content Management System (CMS) we build One Day Websites in. We have purposely chosen this CMS as it's exceptionally easy to use, edit, and looks great. We love it so much that our own Helium website — the website you're on right now — is built in Duda!


      Duda is a code-free CMS, which means you'll never need to hire technical experts or Developers to help you. You can have full control of the website once the build is complete and won't need to rely on us for future updates. Of course, we're always available to help you if you need us!

    • SEO

      SEO stands for Search Engine Optimisation. It's the process of getting your website picked up by search engines, like Google, with the goal of appearing on the first page of search engine results. SEO is an extremely time intensive (but worth it) marketing strategy that takes months of work before you start seeing results. 


      However, we implement basic SEO during your One Day Website build to help you get a head start. We can also provide on-going SEO to help you stand out on Google, if that's something that's a priority for you.

    • What’s the average cost for a small business website?

      There’s a wide range of cost points for small business websites. From this website pricing guide and this website cost article, you can see that small business website costs range from $5k to $10k, and $1k to $10k (respectively).


      The size of the website and whether you hire a professional (or a team of professionals) are two main factors involved. For example, a website is often cheaper when you hire a one-man-band, but the chances of them being exeptional in all the required areas — copy, design, development, and project management — is extremely unlikely. Athough you're paying less, you might end up with a sub-par website that shines in one area, but is lacking in others. That's one of the main reasons people love engaging Helium for a Simple Website — you're paying for a whole team!

    • Why does a small business need a website?

      There are heaps of reasons why small business owners should have a website. The main one is, your website is a central landing point for all potential customers.


      Not everyone is ready to call straight away, so this gives customers the opportunity to do a discreet credibility check while they’re considering whether to get in touch. It's also a place where you can share your amazing services, team expertise, benefits, and points of difference.


      Your website also houses all your contact information, as well as blogs (if you need them).

    • What website maintenance will I need to do?

      None, we do it for you!

    • What do I need to do to prepare for my Simple Website?

      Once you sign up and pay a 50% deposit (which secures your booking), you'll be linked to a simple online briefing form where you can tell us all about your business. Filling this in is basically all you need to do before we can start the build. After that, all you need to do is be available. 


      The briefing form will ask you about the business, your points of difference, the colours you'd prefer to see, as well as the general "vibe" you're aspiring to. You're invited to reference other website you like to look of. If you have a website already, you can share what you do and don't like about it. If you have a logo or style guide, you'll be asked to supply that to us, and if you have any photos that you'd like us to include, you can send those through, too. 

    • How many people can I bring along to Simple Website meetings?

      There’s no hard limit, but one-to-three people (including yourself) is about right. Mostly, we see just one or two people, sometimes we have as many as six. 


      If it helps you at all, the more people there are, the longer the project will take, and possibly more complex it will be. This is because there are many different — and often conflicting — ideas and opinions, and this can really hold the project up (espeically if it's a One Day Website). 


      If you are bringing someone, it’s a good idea to make it clear who is responsible for making the final calls. For example, if you and your team really can't agree on something, nominate one person who has the final word. Getting stuck in decision-limbo makes is difficult for us to move forward as we get mixed or inconsistent feedback, so this will help us (and you!) out. 


      And remember, you can continue discussing the website with your team after the project, and edit it yourself with whatever you guys come up with.
